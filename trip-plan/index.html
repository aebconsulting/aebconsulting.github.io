Part 1 — Pisgah National Forest Waterfalls (US-276)

All three waterfalls are sequential along US-276 heading north through Pisgah National Forest — zero backtracking.

  • 1. Looking Glass Falls — 60 ft (Roadside) The star attraction. Walk down ~60 steps to the base of this stunning 60-ft waterfall. After Monday's storms, water flow will be spectacular. 15–20 min. FREE. Info
  • 2. Moore Cove Falls — 50 ft (1.2 mi RT) Walk BEHIND a 50-ft waterfall through lush forest! Kid favorite — magical core-memory experience. Easy trail, 30–45 min. 1 mi north of Looking Glass on US-276. AllTrails
  • 3. Sliding Rock — 60 ft natural waterslide (Roadside) Natural 60-ft rock waterslide with 11,000 gallons/min flowing over it. Too cold to slide in March but amazing to watch & photograph. Quick 10–15 min stop. 3 mi north of Moore Cove on US-276. Info
Tip: These three waterfalls are all along one road (US-276 north). After Monday’s storms, water flow will be roaring. Moore Cove Falls is the star — walking behind a waterfall is a core memory for Ethan. Bring a jacket for the mist!

Part 2 — Blue Ridge Parkway Scenic Drive

After Sliding Rock, US-276 continues north and intersects the Blue Ridge Parkway at Milepost 411.8. The section from MP 393.6 to MP 420.2 is OPEN (confirmed post-Helene). This is a gorgeous mountain ridge drive with stunning views.

  • Scenic Drive: MP 411.8 south to MP 417 (~5 mi) Turn south on the BRP from US-276. Drive along the ridge at 35–45 mph with mountain panoramas on both sides. Allow 15–20 min with a pullover or two for photos. Snow-dusted peaks after Monday’s storm!
  • Skinny Dip Falls (BRP MP 417) — Optional Bonus Waterfall Cascading falls with wading pools in deep forest. 1 mi round trip, moderate. 30–45 min if energy allows. Small parking area at MP 417 — look for the trailhead sign on the east side.
  • Looking Glass Rock Overlook (MP 417) Iconic view of the massive granite dome — one of the most photographed spots on the BRP. Right at the Skinny Dip Falls parking area. Quick stop even if you skip the hike.
BRP status (confirmed): The NPS completed the landslide repair at MP 401.5 (Ferrin Knob Tunnel), restoring 38 continuous miles from MP 382 to MP 420. Our scenic section (MP 411.8–417) is fully within this open corridor. Speed limit is 45 mph max; expect 30–35 mph on curves. You cannot continue south past MP 420 (still closed from Helene). After the scenic drive, return north on BRP to US-276 and head south toward DuPont.
⚠️ Morning-of check: The BRP can close temporarily for snow or ice. After Monday’s storms, check conditions before driving up: NPS Road Status | BRP Conditions

Part 3 — DuPont State Forest Waterfalls

DuPont has three major waterfalls on the Little River, all accessible from the Hooker Falls parking area. Moderate hiking on well-maintained trails.

  • 4. Hooker Falls — 12 ft (0.5 mi RT) Wide, powerful curtain waterfall. Shortest walk from the parking lot — flat, easy path. Great warm-up. Info
  • 5. Triple Falls — 120 ft total (1.4 mi from Hooker Falls) Three cascading drops — featured in The Hunger Games and The Last of the Mohicans. Multiple viewing platforms. Moderate trail. THE must-see at DuPont.
  • 6. High Falls — 150 ft (0.3 mi past Triple Falls) The tallest in DuPont — 150-ft dramatic plunge over a cliff. Viewing platform with railing. Worth the extra walk from Triple Falls.
  • 7. Bridal Veil Falls — 120 ft (0.6 mi past High Falls, optional) Delicate, wispy falls. Only if energy and time allow — the trail continues along the river. Beautiful but not essential.
DuPont tip: The trail from Hooker Falls → Triple Falls → High Falls is a 3.2 mi out-and-back along the Little River. Well-maintained, moderate difficulty. Triple Falls alone is worth the trip — Hunger Games fans will recognize it instantly.

Part 4 — Downtown Brevard

After DuPont, head 15 min to downtown Brevard — a charming mountain town known as the “Land of Waterfalls.” Great for lunch, shopping, and letting Ethan explore.

  • O.P. Taylor’s Toy Store 16 S Broad St — Legendary independent toy store, one of the best in the Southeast! Floor-to-ceiling toys, games, puzzles, stuffed animals. Ethan will go wild. A Brevard must-stop. Website
  • Downtown Main Street & Broad Street Walkable downtown with local shops, galleries, bookstores, and cafes. Ice cream shops, candy stores, and mountain-town charm.
  • White Squirrels Brevard is famous for its colony of white squirrels! Keep an eye out around downtown — they’re real and they’re everywhere.
  • Lunch Options The Square Root — creative sandwiches & salads. Rocky’s Grill — burgers & comfort food. Mayberry’s — classic diner. Or grab pizza and eat by the courthouse lawn.

Part 5 — The Marquee + Late Dinner in Asheville

Instead of heading straight to the cabin from Brevard, take the same route north (US-276 → NC-280 → I-26) but exit into Asheville’s River Arts District for The Marquee and dinner.

  • The Marquee — 36 Foundry St, River Arts District A massive 50,000 sq ft curated indoor marketplace in a converted warehouse. 145+ vendors selling art, antiques, vintage decor, jewelry, crafts, home goods, clothing, and more. European street market meets Asheville creativity. Bar on-site. Open daily 11 AM–6 PM. Website
  • Late Dinner in Asheville The River Arts District and downtown Asheville have endless options. Family-friendly picks: White Duck Taco Shop (creative tacos, right near The Marquee, quick & kid-approved) · 12 Bones Smokehouse (legendary Asheville BBQ, casual counter-service) · Biscuit Head (Southern comfort food, massive cat-head biscuits) · Twisted Laurel (wood-fired pizza, family-friendly patio).
